# AI Biosecurity Risk Evaluation

## From COVID-19 Mortality Analysis to AI Biosecurity Risk Evaluation

This project is designed as a companion to my NYC COVID-19 Mortality Pattern Analysis project. Together, the two repositories explore biological systems thinking, crisis analysis, evidence interpretation, and safety-focused AI evaluation.

- Repo 1: NYC COVID-19 Mortality Pattern Analysis  
  Public health analysis, mortality patterns, investigative epidemiology, and crisis-context scientific reasoning.

- Repo 2: AI Biosecurity Risk Evaluation  
  AI safety, biological misinformation, dual-use reasoning, escalation logic, and expert review of biological content.

The goal of this project is not to generate hazardous biological information. The goal is to demonstrate how a biomedical scientist can evaluate AI-generated biological content for accuracy, ambiguity, misinformation risk, and operational safety concerns.

---

## Project Purpose

Frontier AI systems can produce biological explanations that range from useful educational content to misleading, overconfident, or potentially unsafe guidance.

COVID-19 demonstrated how rapidly biological information can become socially consequential when scientific uncertainty, public fear, misinformation, and rapidly evolving evidence collide. More recently, renewed public attention surrounding hantavirus has highlighted how quickly zoonotic disease discussions can spread online, often mixing accurate public-health guidance with speculation, misunderstanding, and fear amplification.

This project explores how biological AI outputs may be evaluated using structured expert review categories including:

- scientific accuracy,
- misinformation risk,
- dual-use ambiguity,
- operational safety concerns,
- and escalation requirements for expert review.

The framework is intentionally conservative and non-operational. It does not provide procedural biological guidance or hazardous protocols.

---

## Why Expert Biological Review Still Matters

Biological information does not exist in a vacuum. Understanding nuance, uncertainty, and context is one of the hallmarks of biomedical research.

AI systems can generate biologically incorrect or unsupported claims that sound technically convincing. In some cases, these hallucinations remain isolated mistakes requiring subject matter expert correction. In other cases, incorrect information becomes public-health misinformation once distributed widely through social media, online discourse, or AI-generated content pipelines.

COVID-19 demonstrated how rapidly misinformation can spread when confidence exceeds expertise. Public-health misinformation can distort risk perception, influence behavior, erode trust, and amplify panic during periods of scientific uncertainty.

This project argues that expert biological review remains essential because biological risk evaluation requires more than keyword filtering or rigid automation. Human reviewers provide contextual reasoning, mechanistic understanding, escalation judgment, and awareness of downstream public-health consequences.
